# Model Card, Mango Recalcitrant Seed Drying Simulator## Model details**Model name:** Mango Recalcitrant Seed Drying Simulator (Physiology + Injury Proxy)  **Model type:** Mechanistic + statistical simulation (parametric dynamics + noise)  **Primary outputs:** Moisture content, germination, conductivity proxy, respiration proxy across treatments and time.This simulator generates synthetic trajectories for seed drying and viability loss under four treatment conditions:- Air vs sun drying- With vs without endocarp## Intended use### Primary intended uses- Teaching and training in seed physiology data analysis- Prototyping statistical analysis workflows before collecting wet-lab data- Sensitivity testing of experimental designs (sampling times, replicate numbers)- Demonstrating CMC estimation and injury progression analysis### Out-of-scope uses- Predicting real mango seed outcomes without calibration to measured datasets- Clinical, medical, or safety-critical decision making- Making claims about specific cultivars or storage protocols without validation## Inputs and outputs### Inputs- Treatment label (ADWE, ADWO, SDWE, SDWO)- Time (hours)- Replicate index (for noise realization)- Parameter set (drying rate, CMC, slopes, noise levels, optional respiration bump)### OutputsPer sample (treatment ? time ? replicate):- `moisture_pct` (% FW)- `germination_pct` (0Đ100)- `conductivity_uS` (normalized units, proxy for leakage)- `respiration_mgCO2_g_h` (proxy scale)- Derived: `viable`, `PC1`, `PC2`, `cluster`## Modeling assumptions- Moisture follows exponential decay to an asymptote.- Germination follows a logistic dependence on moisture with a threshold-like collapse around CMC.- Conductivity rises as moisture decreases (logistic-inverted function).- Respiration declines with moisture (logistic), optionally with a Gaussian transient bump.- Replicate variability is approximated as Gaussian noise.## EvaluationBecause outputs are simulated, evaluation focuses on:- Internal consistency (monotonic and threshold behavior)- Expected treatment ordering (SDWO dries fastest, ADWE slowest)- Coherence across variables (conductivity increases as germination decreases)Recommended validation for real-world use:- Fit parameters using experimentally measured time courses for a specific cultivar and lab protocol.- Compare predicted and observed CMC, T50, leakage trajectories, and respiration.## Ethical considerations- Synthetic data can be mistaken for real data, all outputs must be clearly labeled as simulated.- Simulation choices can encode biases (e.g., assumed CMC), which must be documented.## Limitations- Does not model within-seed moisture gradients explicitly.- Does not incorporate microbial effects, temperature fluctuations, or oxygen diffusion limits.- Conductivity and respiration units are simplified proxies unless calibrated.## Versioning- v1.0: base simulator + analysis workflow, export of CSVs and figures
